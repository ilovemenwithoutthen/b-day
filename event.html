Events

  • 1933: The 21st Amendment to the United States Constitution is ratified, ending Prohibition.

    Prohibition, which began in 1920, made the production, sale, and transportation of alcoholic beverages illegal in the United States. The 21st Amendment repealed the 18th Amendment, allowing for the legal sale of alcohol once again. This marked a significant shift in American culture and law, leading to the establishment of a regulated alcohol industry.

  • 1945: Flight 19, a group of five TBM Avenger torpedo bombers, disappears over the Bermuda Triangle.

    Flight 19 was a training flight for the U.S. Navy that went missing during a routine mission over the Bermuda Triangle. The disappearance of the five planes and their crew members sparked numerous theories and investigations, contributing to the area's reputation for mysterious disappearances. Despite extensive search efforts, no trace of the planes was ever found.

  • 1989: The first episode of "The Simpsons" airs on Fox.

    "The Simpsons," created by Matt Groening, debuted as a half-hour prime time show on December 5, 1989. The animated sitcom quickly became a cultural phenomenon, known for its satirical take on American life, family dynamics, and social issues. It has since become the longest-running American sitcom and has influenced countless other shows.

  • 1993: The first episode of "The X-Files" airs on Fox.

    "The X-Files," created by Chris Carter, premiered on December 5, 1993. The show follows FBI agents Fox Mulder and Dana Scully as they investigate unsolved cases involving paranormal phenomena. The series gained a massive following and is credited with popularizing the science fiction and horror genres on television, leading to a resurgence of interest in UFOs and conspiracy theories.

  • 2013: Nelson Mandela, former President of South Africa, passes away at the age of 95.

    Nelson Mandela was a key figure in the struggle against apartheid in South Africa and became the country's first black president from 1994 to 1999. His leadership and commitment to reconciliation and social justice earned him global respect and admiration. His death on December 5, 2013, was met with an outpouring of tributes and remembrances from around the world.
